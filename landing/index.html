Stop finding out credentials
expired in production.

Tribal keeps track of your team's certificates, API keys, and secrets — who owns them, when they expire, and how to rotate them. It notifies you before things break, not after.

TLS Certificates API Keys SSH Keys Slack Alerts Audit Trail Terraform Provider Open Source
🔔

Know before it breaks

Tribal sends Slack reminders on a schedule you control — days or weeks before a credential expires. The right team gets notified in the right channel, not just the person who set it up.

🔑

Stop starting from scratch

Paste an API key and Tribal identifies the provider, looks up the expiry where possible, and pre-fills rotation steps. Works with GitHub, Stripe, AWS, Azure, GCP, OpenAI, Anthropic, Cloudflare, Fastly, Slack, and more.

📋

Always know who's responsible

Every resource has a designated owner, a purpose, and rotation instructions. No more hunting through Slack history or hoping the person who set it up still works there.

📅

See what's coming

A visual calendar shows every upcoming expiry at a glance — monthly or year-at-a-glance. Color-coded by urgency so you can see trouble at a distance.

🔍

Full audit trail

Every change is logged — who created it, who updated it, who deleted it, and when. Useful for compliance, handoffs, and post-mortems.

🔄

Keep credentials reviewed

Configure a review cadence and Tribal flags anything that hasn't been looked at recently — even credentials that don't technically expire but still need attention.

🤖

Fits into your existing workflow

Full REST API so you can integrate Tribal into CI pipelines, internal tooling, or scripts. A Terraform provider lets you manage credentials alongside the infrastructure that uses them.

📄

Shareable reports

Generate PDF snapshots of any resource with full details and audit history. Export CSV reports of upcoming expiries and recent changes for compliance reviews or team handoffs.

🌱

Open source

Tribal is open source and self-hostable. Run it on your own infrastructure, keep your data where it belongs, and contribute providers for the tools your team uses.
